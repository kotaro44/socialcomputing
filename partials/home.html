Demographics Questions

Thank You!



You will be asked to participate in a study. We are asking you to take part because you responded to our flyer notices posted on campus, or our SONA email.

What we will ask you to do
You will be shown approximately 10-15 different mobile app description/ratings pages for social, game, and health apps. After each one, you will be asked to rate the app on several measures such as how much you might be interested in using it or recommending it to a friend. We will then ask you some questions about your internet and mobile phone use. Afterwards, you will be asked to fill out some general demographic questions.

Purpose of this Study
We’re interested in learning what types of mobile applications (health, gaming, social) college students use, like, and are likely to recommend to friends.

Expected Duration
The expected total duration of the study is 30 minutes, and can be taken online or in 494 Mann (Sue Fussell’s Intercultural Communication Lab at Mann Library’s Department of Communication).

Participant Requirements
Participants must be at least 18 years old to participate. They must also own and regularly use a smartphone.

Risks
The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during the performance of routine physical or psychological examinations or tests.

Rights
Your participation is voluntary. You are free to stop your participation at any point. Refusal to participate or withdrawal of your consent or discontinued participation in the study will not result in any penalty. The Principal Investigator may at his/her discretion remove you from the study for any of a number of reasons. In such an event, you will not suffer any penalty. It will not affect your current or future relationship with Cornell University.

Benefits
There may be no personal benefit from your participation in the study but the research will contribute to a better understanding of college students’ use and perceptions of different types of mobile applications.

Compensation & Costs
You will receive 0.5 SONA credits for completing this study. There will be no cost to you if you participate in this study. Compensation is based on study completion and while you are free to withdraw at any time, credit cannot be given for withdrawals.

Confidentiality
Your consent form and data will be confidential. Your identity will be kept confidential throughout the study. Any data collected in the study will only be accessible to the researchers involved. When publishing the results, we will not include any information that will make it possible to identify you.

Because the recruitment of participants is facilitated by using email and Internet, the message communicated via Internet could be read by a third party due to the nature of Internet technologies. However, the researchers will not intentionally disclose any communication contents to any third parties.

Right to Ask Questions & Contact Information
The researchers conducting this study are Shruti Sannon, a PhD student in Communication at Cornell University and Christine Geeng and Shayra Kamal, undergraduate students in Information Science. If you have any questions, you may contact Shruti Sannon at ss3464@cornell.edu, Christine Geeng at cg447@cornell.edu, and/or Shayra Kamal at sk2328@cornell.edu.

You can reach the overseeing faculty member, Prof. Susan Fussell, at sfussell@cornell.edu. If you have any questions or concerns regarding your rights as a subject in this study, you may contact the Institutional Review Board (IRB) at 607-255-5138 or access their website at http://www.irb.cornell.edu. You may also report your concerns or complaints anonymously through Ethicspoint (http://www.hotline.cornell.edu.) or by calling toll free at 1-866-293-3077. Ethics point is an independent organization that serves as a liaison between the University and the person bringing the complaint so that anonymity can be ensured.
